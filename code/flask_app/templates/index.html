Our Objective

This project aims to create two-way communication bridges, shattering accessibility barriers for individuals with visual, auditory, and reading challenges. Through a technological trilogy - Visuals to Text, Sign Language Translation, and Text-to-Speech/Speech-to-Text - we unlock information, ignite expression, and foster empathy between diverse communities. Together, we weave a more inclusive world where every voice becomes a vibrant thread in the human tapestry.

Our Functionalities

Visual Question Answering

This feature is like having a seeing-eye AI! Imagine describing a scene and getting answers to your questions. It uses image recognition and language processing to understand both the image and your query. For blind people, VQA can be a game-changer. By describing their surroundings, they can get details about objects, colors, and even people, making daily tasks and navigation easier and more independent. So, VQA isn't just answering questions, it's opening doors to a richer world for those who can't see it themselves.

Button
Sign language processing

This Module bridges the communication gap by translating between sign language and spoken language. Think of it as a real-time interpreter in your pocket! For text-to-sign, it transforms written words into understandable signs, aiding deaf individuals in accessing information and conversations. Sign-to-text, on the other hand, empowers deafblind individuals by converting their signs into text, enabling them to express themselves and connect with the world. SLP isn't just processing signs, it's unlocking communication and breaking down barriers for those who rely on sign language.

Button
Text recognition/speech to text conversion

Imagine the world narrated to you or your voice instantly transformed into text. That's the power of image-to-speech and speech-to-text!. Image-to-speech (using OCR) reads the world for you. It uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to turn pictures into spoken words. Text on signs, documents, or packaging becomes accessible, empowering blind people to navigate independently and gain information on their own. Speech-to-text gives voice to your thoughts. It converts spoken words into written text, allowing blind people to communicate easily, participate in conversations, and even write messages or emails.
